What's a question you've never stopped asking?

We live in a world full of answers. I'm more interested in the questions.

This is where I keep ideas that won't leave me alone, observations that don't fit anywhere else, and conversations I'd like to have more often. Some are about work. Most aren't.

I have a feeling there are a lot of people carrying around thoughts they never get to finish, questions they never get to ask, and stories they never get to tell — not because they aren't worth sharing, but because they never found the right audience.

Consider this a place for those things.

If you have a question you can't shake, an observation that changed how you see something, or a thought that deserves more than a passing conversation — send it my way. If it's interesting enough, we'll pull on the thread together and see where it leads.

Work with me

I enjoy conversations with people who are building, learning, experimenting, or trying to solve difficult problems. The kind of problems where the answer isn't in a framework and the person asking it already knows the obvious answers.

Business consulting

If you're running a business and something isn't working — growth, revenue, how the sales function is structured — I'm happy to take a look. I've spent seven years in education building and rebuilding these things. I know what moves numbers and what just looks like it does.

I work best on problems that have already been thought about seriously, not ones that need defining from scratch. If you've been sitting with something for a while and want a different pair of eyes, that's usually a good starting point.

Student consulting

I work with students — not as a tutor, more as a thinking partner for someone trying to figure out what to do next. MBA applications, career pivots, going abroad for the first time, choosing between two paths that both seem reasonable.

I've sat with most of these questions enough to be useful. I try not to give advice that sounds good but doesn't actually help.

Pro bono

From time to time I take on work without charging for it. If you're building something in education and don't have the budget for proper consulting, reach out anyway. I'd rather spend an afternoon on something worthwhile than pass on it for a reason that won't matter in a year.

I also occasionally contribute time to projects I find worthwhile — causes, initiatives, ideas that are genuinely trying to do something. Not everything has to involve money.

If any of this sounds relevant, email is the right place to start. A short note about what you're working on is enough.

About

Original

I'm Shradha, I usually go by Shra.

I've spent seven years in education — not teaching, but building: sales teams, revenue lines, new markets. The kind of work where you find out very quickly whether something actually works or just sounds like it should.

I take on consulting work when the problem is interesting enough. I'm selective about it, not because I'm oversubscribed, but because bad fits waste everyone's time. If you're building something real and need someone who will tell you what they actually think, that's when this works.

Outside of work: I travel when I can, read more than I finish, and think often about why good advice is so hard to give and even harder to take.
